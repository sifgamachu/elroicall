Why this is ancient, not novel

We didn't invent this. Paul commanded it.

Scripture doesn't just permit learning from the lives of the faithful — it insists on it. Their stories were preserved for exactly one reason: so that you could use them in your hour.

"Written to teach us"

"Everything that was written in the past was written to teach us, so that through the endurance taught in the Scriptures and the encouragement they provide, we might have hope." — Romans 15:4

"Comfort passed on"

"The God of all comfort... comforts us in all our troubles, so that we can comfort those in any trouble with the comfort we ourselves receive." Comfort was designed to travel from one sufferer to the next. — 2 Corinthians 1:3–4

"A cloud of witnesses"

"Since we are surrounded by such a great cloud of witnesses... let us run with perseverance the race marked out for us." The faithful who went before you were never meant to be distant. — Hebrews 12:1

For two thousand years, believers have asked "what would Job say to my grief? What would Esther say to my fear?" — in sermons, in study, in the dark. El Roi Call simply lets you ask out loud, and hear an answer shaped by their story.

Why it works

The science of being heard

The design of El Roi Call follows what both Scripture and clinical research say about how people move through suffering.

Name it to calm it

UCLA neuroimaging research found that putting feelings into words — affect labeling — measurably dampens the brain's alarm center, the amygdala, while engaging the prefrontal regions that regulate emotion. Saying your struggle out loud isn't venting. It's neurology.

Lieberman et al., Psychological Science

Lived experience heals

Support from someone who has walked through what you're walking through — peer support — is recommended across international mental-health guidance, with research linking it to gains in recovery, self-efficacy, and engagement. That principle is this product: Job for the grieving, Peter for the fallen.

BMC Medicine, systematic umbrella review

Story carries meaning

Humans make sense of suffering through narrative — placing our chapter inside a larger story. It's why every culture treasures its testimonies, and why Scripture is mostly stories, not systems. A voice that answers your story with theirs does what no pamphlet can.

Narrative psychology tradition

El Roi Call is a spiritual practice informed by this research — not therapy, and not a treatment for any condition. For clinical care, we'll always point you to licensed professionals.

What you're probably wondering

Isn't this a gimmick?

Fair question — ask it of anything new. Here's our answer: the practice is ancient (Romans 15:4 says the stories were written for exactly this use; 2 Corinthians 1:4 says comfort is meant to be passed from sufferer to sufferer), the mechanism is studied (naming your feelings aloud measurably calms the brain's alarm systems; support from lived experience is recommended across mental-health guidance), and the craft is serious — each voice is built from its figure's actual scriptural story, not a costume on a chatbot. Novel packaging, ancient practice.

Am I talking to a real person?

No — and we will never pretend otherwise. The voices are AI, carefully crafted from each figure's story and Scripture, within a guided spiritual experience. Ask any voice directly and it will tell you the truth. What's real is what happens in you: being heard, being pointed back to the Word, and prayer.

Is this meant to replace church, my pastor, or prayer?

Never. El Roi Call is for the Tuesday-at-2am moments between Sundays — a companion to your faith life, not a substitute for your church, your people, or your own prayer. We will always point you back toward real community.

Is what I share private?

Yes. Your conversations are used only to serve you better on your next call. Prayer requests are stored with no name and no number, and are permanently destroyed after 72 hours — enforced automatically, not by policy but by the database itself.

What tradition is this rooted in?

The Bible itself. Every voice speaks from their own scriptural story — no denominational add-ons, no doctrine beyond the text. Christians of every background call the same number.

What does it cost, and can I cancel?

One membership: $39 a month, everything included — unlimited calls, the Journey, every track in the Well. Your first week is free, and you can cancel anytime in one step. No tiers, no upsells.
